Here is a complete, birds-eye-view roadmap of exactly what your project is going to do, what your models will predict, and which models will be used where.

As outlined in your project blueprint, this project is a Neuro-Symbolic Spatial-Temporal visibility forecasting system. It combines advanced machine learning ("the Neuro Brain") with mathematically rigid physical weather laws ("the Symbolic Guardrail").

1. What is your model going to predict?
Your models will predict three key targets:

Target Variable: Ground-truth IGI Runway Visibility (airport_visibility) in meters (ranging from 0 to 10,000 meters).
Forecasting Horizon: Forward-looking visibility predictions from $t+1$ to $t+6$ hours into the future, using historical patterns from the preceding 24 hours.
Visibility Collapse Events: Probabilistic predictions flagging sudden drops in runway visibility below safety-critical aviation operation thresholds (specifically $< 800$ meters and $< 500$ meters).
2. Which models will be used, and where?
To maximize the validity of your project, we will build a Neuro-Symbolic Architecture split into two distinct layers:

Layer A: The Connectionist Layer ("The Neuro Brain")
This layer is responsible for taking your 50 engineered physical features and predicting the forward visibility horizons. We will train and benchmark three distinct archetypes:

Baseline Regressors (Random Forests / XGBoost / LightGBM):
Where: Used to establish cross-sectional feature importance baselines and identify which thermodynamic/aerosol columns have the highest mathematical impact on visibility.
Sequence Time-Series Networks (LSTMs or GRUs):
Where: Used to capture chronological weather trends and the temporal progression of fog and moisture accumulation over the previous 24-hour window.
Spatial-Temporal Benchmarks (Conv-LSTM or Temporal Fusion Transformers):
Where: Used to map time-series trends and spatial correlations across the three weather stations (IGI Airport, Safdarjung urban, and Rohtak rural) concurrently.
Layer B: The Deterministic Boundary Layer ("The Symbolic Guardrail")
This is the core scientific contribution of your project. Rather than trusting "black-box" machine learning predictions blindly, we build a mathematical guardrail:

Tool: Microsoft Research's Z3 SMT (Satisfiability Modulo Theories) Solver.
Where: It intercepts the deep neural network’s predictive outputs at runtime and cross-examines them against deterministic physical weather boundaries.
Example: If the neural network predicts thick fog ($<500$m) but the dry air temperature is $20^\circ\text{C}$ warmer than the dew point, the Z3 solver flags this prediction as physically impossible (a model hallucination), blocks it, and automatically overrides it with a mathematically sound, physics-safe fallback condition.
